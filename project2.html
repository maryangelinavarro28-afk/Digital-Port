III. DEFINITION OF TERMS:

1. Mean - The mean is the average value of a set of numbers. It tells us the central or typical value in a data set.



2. Variance - Variance is the expected value of the squared variation of a random variable from its mean value, in probability and statistics.


3. Standard Deviation - A statistical measure of variability that indicates the average amount that a set of numbers deviates from their mean.

II. Optimal Angle


The optimal angle for maximum distance in projectile motion is 45°, because it provides the perfect balance between height and horizontal range. At 30°, the shot is lower and faster but shorter; at 60°, the ball flies higher but lands sooner.




III. Projectile Motion


Projectile motion affects how far a golf ball travels after being hit, and the launch angle plays a big role. A 45° angle usually gives the best balance of height and distance, while 30° makes the shot lower and longer, and 60° sends the ball higher but shorter. In real golf, the best angle is often between 10° and 20° because of air resistance, backspin, and club design. By studying practice swings and ball flight, golfers can figure out the most effective angle to achieve maximum distance on the course.




IV. Conclusion


While the optimal driver angle is 10-12 degrees, our case study proves that for the higher angles we tested, a lower trajectory is better for distance. The 30° angle was the most effective for carry, while the 45° and 60° angles were progressively less so. This confirms that for distance, a lower flight path is more efficient.
