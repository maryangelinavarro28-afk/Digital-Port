II. INTRODUCTION:

Free Fall is an object that moves due to the action of no other force rather than the force of gravity. Free fall usually starts from at rest and it travels due to the pull of gravity. In this case, the object’s initial velocity is equal to zero and it changes to its maximum shortly before it strikes the ground. Because of this phenomenon, a freely falling body is changing its velocity constantly per unit of time. This is what is called uniformly accelerated motion.

For a unifom accelerated motion, the relation between distance (d) travel by the free fall, it’s velocity (vi) and the time 9t) is expressed by:

d = vi t + ½ at
